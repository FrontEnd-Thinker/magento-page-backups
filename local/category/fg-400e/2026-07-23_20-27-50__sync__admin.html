FortiGate 400E / 401E at a Glance

Performance

Firewall throughput reaches up to 32 Gbps on large packets and 24 Gbps on 64-byte traffic, with 36 Mpps packet forwarding and 2.14 microsecond latency. IPS runs at 7.8 Gbps, NGFW at 6 Gbps, and full threat protection at 5 Gbps, all measured with logging active.

Capacity

Supports 4,000,000 concurrent TCP sessions and 450,000 new sessions per second. IPsec VPN throughput reaches 20 Gbps with up to 50,000 client-to-gateway tunnels. SSL-VPN delivers 4.5 Gbps with 5,000 concurrent tunnel-mode users. Application control tops out at 12 Gbps.

Connectivity

Ships with 16x hardware-accelerated GE RJ45 ports, 16x GE SFP slots, and 2x GE RJ45 management ports (MGMT and HA, dedicated). Two USB ports and one console port complete the front panel. The 401E adds 2x 240 GB onboard SSD for local log storage.

Platform

1U rackmount chassis, 1.75 x 17.0 x 15.0 inches, with a single hot-swappable AC power supply included and a second slot for an optional redundant PSU. Supports up to 72 FortiSwitches and 512 FortiAPs via FortiLink. Active-Active, Active-Passive, and clustering HA modes are all supported.

FortiGate 400E / 401E FAQ

The FortiGate 400E and 401E share the same hardware platform, SPU processors, port configuration, and performance figures. The 401E adds 2x 240 GB of onboard SSD storage for local logging, which is useful in environments where sending all log data to a central FortiAnalyzer is not practical. A third variant, the 401E-DC, is identical to the 401E but ships with a DC power supply for environments running DC power infrastructure.

Fortinet positions the 400E series for mid-sized to large enterprise deployments at the campus edge or enterprise branch level. With 4 million concurrent sessions, 450,000 new sessions per second, and 32 Gbps of firewall throughput, these appliances handle environments with several hundred to a few thousand users without running into headroom problems. Organizations that need SD-WAN, ZTNA, and full NGFW services running simultaneously on the same box will find the 400E series capable of running all three without throughput trade-offs.
