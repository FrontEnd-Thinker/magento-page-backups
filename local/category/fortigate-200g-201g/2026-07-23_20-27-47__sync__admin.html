FortiGate 200G / 201G at a Glance

Performance

Firewall throughput of 39 Gbps at 1518-byte and 512-byte packet sizes, with 26.5 Gbps at 64 bytes. IPsec VPN throughput reaches 36 Gbps. New sessions per second: 400,000. Firewall latency runs 4.36 microseconds. Application Control throughput: 27.8 Gbps. CAPWAP: 37.5 Gbps. NP7Lite and CP10 ASICs handle all packet and content acceleration without CPU involvement.

Threat Protection

IPS with enterprise traffic mix and logging active: 9 Gbps. NGFW (firewall, IPS, and Application Control combined): 7 Gbps. Full Threat Protection with Malware Protection added: 6 Gbps. SSL Inspection with IPS: 7 Gbps, covering TLS 1.3. SSL Inspection CPS: 7,100. SSL-VPN throughput: 3 Gbps with a recommended 500 concurrent tunnel users.

Interfaces

8x GE RJ45 switch ports, 1x MGMT RJ45, 1x HA RJ45, 8x 5/2.5/GE RJ45, 4x GE SFP slots, 8x 10GE SFP+/SFP FortiLink slots, 1x USB, 1x console. The 201G adds 480 GB SSD for onboard log storage. FortiLink supports up to 64 FortiSwitches and up to 256 FortiAPs with 128 in tunnel mode.

HA and Management

Active-Active, Active-Passive, and Clustering HA all supported. Up to 10 virtual domains. FortiManager-ready for centralized multi-device policy management. FortiAnalyzer-ready for logging and analytics. Holds up to 5,000 FortiTokens. Both models ship with dual non-hot-swappable AC power supplies for 1+1 redundancy.

FortiGate 200G / 201G FAQ

The hardware specs are identical on both models. Same port layout, same NP7Lite and CP10 ASICs, same throughput ratings, and dual AC power supplies on both. The only difference is storage. The 200G ships without an onboard drive. The 201G includes a 480 GB SSD for local log storage and reporting. If you plan to forward logs to FortiAnalyzer or an external SIEM, the 200G works fine. If you want local log retention without a separate logging device, the 201G saves you the extra purchase.

With the full service stack running, including firewall, IPS, Application Control, and Malware Protection, the 200G delivers 6 Gbps of Threat Protection throughput. IPS on its own runs at 9 Gbps. NGFW throughput, which combines firewall, IPS, and Application Control, hits 7 Gbps. Raw firewall throughput is 39 Gbps at 1518-byte and 512-byte packet sizes. All performance numbers are measured with logging active, which is how real networks operate.

Fortinet rates the 200G series for 400 to 500 concurrent users. The 11 million concurrent session table and 400,000 new sessions per second give the platform enough headroom for that user range, including environments with active SSL inspection and a heavy HTTPS traffic mix.

The appliance hardware ships without a FortiGuard subscription. You add the services that match your environment. The most common bundle includes IPS, Application Control, Web Filtering, Antivirus and Anti-Botnet Security, and Zero-Day Threat Prevention. FortiCare support covers firmware updates and TAC access. Firewalls.com quotes hardware, FortiGuard services, and FortiCare together so you have one number, not three separate line items to track down.
