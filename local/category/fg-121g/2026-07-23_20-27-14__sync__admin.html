FortiGate 121G at a Glance

Performance

Firewall throughput of 39 Gbps (1518-byte UDP). IPS throughput of 5.3 Gbps. NGFW throughput of 3.1 Gbps. Full threat protection throughput of 2.8 Gbps with firewall, IPS, application control, and malware protection all active. SSL inspection throughput of 3 Gbps. Application control throughput of 6.7 Gbps. Firewall latency of 3.17 microseconds.

Interfaces

16 hardware-accelerated GE RJ45 switch ports. 8 GE SFP slots. 4 x 10GE SFP+ FortiLink slots, reconfigurable as standard ports. 1 dedicated GE RJ45 management port. 1 dedicated GE RJ45 HA port. 1 USB port. 1 RJ45 console port. Bluetooth Low Energy (BLE) for out-of-band management. TPM (Trusted Platform Module) included.

Capacity and VPN

3 million concurrent TCP sessions. 140,000 new sessions per second. 10,000 firewall policies. 2,000 gateway-to-gateway IPsec VPN tunnels. 16,000 client-to-gateway IPsec tunnels. 35 Gbps IPsec VPN throughput (512-byte, AES256-SHA256). 1.5 Gbps SSL-VPN throughput. Up to 500 concurrent SSL-VPN users in tunnel mode. 5,000 FortiTokens supported.

Hardware and Deployment

1RU rack-mount form factor, 1.73 x 17 x 10 inches. SP5 custom ASIC hardware acceleration (121G only over 120G: 480 GB onboard SSD included). Dual built-in, non-hot-swappable AC power supplies for 1+1 redundancy. 43W average power draw. Side-to-back airflow. Active-Active and Active-Passive high availability clustering. Supports up to 32 FortiSwitches and 128 FortiAPs.

FortiGate 121G FAQ

The hardware is nearly identical. The key difference is onboard storage. The 121G ships with a 480 GB SSD, which the 120G does not include. That storage enables local log retention, SD-WAN analytics, and reporting without requiring a separate FortiAnalyzer appliance at the site. Both models share the same port configuration, SP5 ASIC, throughput specs, and FortiOS feature set. If local storage matters for your deployment, the 121G is the right pick.

The SP5 is Fortinet's latest Security Processing Unit, combining a RISC-based CPU with a proprietary content and network processor on a single chip. It accelerates firewall, VPN, and deep packet inspection natively in silicon, which is why the 121G can reach 39 Gbps of raw firewall throughput and 2.8 Gbps of full threat protection without relying on general-purpose CPUs. You get enterprise-grade inspection performance from a 1RU branch appliance without paying for a much larger chassis.

For most branch and distributed enterprise deployments, the UTP (Unified Threat Protection) bundle is the right starting point. It covers IPS, application control, web and DNS filtering, antivirus, and antispam, plus FortiCare Premium support. Organizations with stricter compliance needs or zero-day exposure often step up to the Enterprise Protection bundle, which adds inline sandbox, CASB, DLP, and OT security services. Our engineers can walk you through which bundle maps to your requirements before you buy.
