FortiGate 200F / 201F at a Glance

Performance

Firewall throughput of 27 Gbps on 1518-byte packets, with IPS at 5 Gbps, NGFW at 3.5 Gbps, and Threat Protection at 3 Gbps. Application control runs at 13 Gbps and IPsec VPN hits 13 Gbps. All figures are hardware-accelerated by the NP6XLite network processor and CP9 content processor, so full-inspection throughput does not degrade under real-world load.

Interfaces and Ports

16x GE RJ45 switch ports with dedicated MGMT and HA ports. 8x GE SFP slots for fiber uplinks. 4x 10GE SFP+ slots, including 2 FortiLink-designated slots for direct FortiSwitch stacking. USB and console ports round out the panel. Hardware TPM and Bluetooth Low Energy (BLE) support zero-touch provisioning workflows.

VPN and Remote Access

IPsec VPN throughput of 13 Gbps supports up to 2,000 site-to-site tunnels and 16,000 client-to-gateway tunnels. SSL-VPN delivers 2 Gbps with up to 500 concurrent tunnel-mode users. SSL inspection reaches 4 Gbps at 3,500 connections per second and 300,000 concurrent sessions, so encrypted traffic receives full inspection without a throughput penalty.

Capacity and High Availability

3 million concurrent TCP sessions and 280,000 new sessions per second handle demanding enterprise workloads. Supports 10,000 firewall policies, up to 64 managed FortiSwitches, 256 FortiAPs, and 5,000 FortiTokens. High availability options include Active-Active, Active-Passive, and full clustering. ICSA Labs certified for Firewall, IPsec, IPS, AV, SSL-VPN, and IPv6.

FortiGate 200F / 201F FAQ

The FG-200F and FG-201F share the same hardware platform, port count, and throughput specs. The only hardware difference is internal storage. The FG-201F includes 480 GB of onboard SSD, which enables local log retention and on-box reporting without a separate FortiAnalyzer appliance. The FG-200F has no internal storage and sends logs to an external log management system or FortiCloud. Both models use the same NP6XLite and CP9 hardware acceleration.

Fortinet rates the 200F series for 200 to 300 concurrent users, which makes it a strong fit for mid-sized enterprise locations, large campus sites, and high-traffic branch offices. The 3 million concurrent session capacity and 280,000 new sessions per second give it plenty of headroom for demanding enterprise workloads without running into a hardware ceiling.

Yes. Secure SD-WAN is built into FortiOS and included on every FortiGate at no additional license cost. There is no separate SD-WAN module or subscription required. The NP6XLite processor handles SD-WAN path-steering decisions at line rate, so adding SD-WAN policies does not reduce overall firewall throughput. FortiGate SD-WAN supports application-aware routing, dynamic WAN link health monitoring, and centralized orchestration through FortiManager.

Three primary FortiGuard bundles are available for the 200F and 201F. UTP (Unified Threat Protection) covers IPS, antivirus, botnet, mobile malware, and application control. Advanced Threat Protection adds inline sandbox detection on top of IPS and AV. Enterprise Protection is the most comprehensive tier, covering everything in UTP plus inline CASB, Data Loss Prevention, DNS and video filtering, antispam, and the FortiConverter migration service. All bundles include FortiCare Premium support with next-business-day hardware replacement. Terms of 1, 3, or 5 years are available for each bundle.

Both the FG-200F and FG-201F are 1U rack-mount appliances measuring 1.73 x 17.01 x 13.47 inches (44 x 432 x 342 mm). Both include dual built-in power supplies. A rack mount tray accessory is available separately for installations that require it. The compact 1U footprint fits standard 19-inch rack infrastructure at campus data centers and large branch office wiring closets without requiring any additional rack space.
