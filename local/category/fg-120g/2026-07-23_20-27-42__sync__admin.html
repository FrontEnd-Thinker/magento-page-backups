FortiGate 120G and 121G at a Glance

Performance

Firewall throughput reaches 39 Gbps, with IPS at 5.3 Gbps, NGFW at 3.1 Gbps, and threat protection at 2.8 Gbps. SSL inspection runs at 3 Gbps. The appliance supports up to 3 million concurrent sessions and 300,000 new sessions per second, all driven by the FortiSP5 ASIC without degrading inspection quality.

Interfaces and Storage

Both models ship with 18 Gigabit RJ45 ports (including 1 MGMT, 1 HA, and 16 FortiLink-capable switch ports), 8 Gigabit SFP slots, and 4 shared 10GE SFP+ slots. The 121G adds 480 GB of onboard SSD storage for local logging, reporting, and packet capture. Both include dual built-in AC power supplies for mission-critical redundancy.

Security and SD-WAN

FortiOS delivers full NGFW, SD-WAN, and ZTNA enforcement in a single OS with no add-on license required for those features. AI and ML-powered FortiGuard services cover intrusion prevention, application control, URL filtering, DNS filtering, anti-malware, anti-spam, and inline CASB. A built-in Trusted Platform Module (TPM) protects keys and device integrity at the hardware level.

Management and Fabric

The FortiSP5 SoC integrates a RISC CPU with hardware SPU acceleration for IPsec VPN and SSL inspection. FortiLink ports fold FortiSwitch into the firewall as a logical LAN extension and can be reconfigured as standard ports when not needed. Centralized policy, analytics, and automation are available through FortiManager and FortiAnalyzer, with the full Fortinet Security Fabric supported from day one.

FortiGate 120G and 121G FAQ

The FortiGate 120G and 121G share the same SP5 platform, chassis, port layout, and performance figures. The only difference is onboard storage. The 121G includes a 480 GB SSD for local logging, reporting, and packet capture on the device itself. The 120G has no internal storage. Both ship with dual built-in AC power supplies and identical throughput ratings. If you need local log retention without a separate FortiAnalyzer, choose the 121G.

The FortiGate 120G and 121G deliver 39 Gbps of firewall throughput, 5.3 Gbps of IPS throughput, 3.1 Gbps NGFW throughput, and 2.8 Gbps of full threat protection. Those numbers place this appliance in mid-size campus networks, distributed enterprise branch sites, and data-center-edge deployments where dense Gigabit switch ports and 10GE SFP+ uplinks are needed alongside full NGFW inspection. For smaller branches, the 90G desktop is a step down. For higher session capacity or more throughput, the 200G is the next model up.

The FortiSP5 is Fortinet's fifth-generation Security Processing Unit, a custom ASIC that integrates a RISC system CPU and a dedicated hardware SPU on a single chip. It offloads and accelerates IPsec VPN encryption, SSL inspection, and flow-based threat scanning in hardware rather than software. The practical effect is that the firewall can run full NGFW inspection at rated throughput without the performance cliff that software-only processing creates under load. Most competing appliances in this class rely on general-purpose CPUs, which slow down under deep inspection. The SP5 does not.
